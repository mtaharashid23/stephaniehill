About Stephanie

Stage IV Cancer Survivor.
Writer. Advocate. Speaker.

"Even in the darkest moments, healing and meaning are still possible."

I am a survivor of stage IV thyroid cancer. I am also a writer and an advocate for people navigating the intersection of illness, grief, and recovery. My journey, captured in Healing Tears, began with years of overlooked symptoms and medical dismissal, culminating in a life-threatening thyroid storm. A Stage IV cancer diagnosis, and an eight-hour surgery that changed everything.

On the same day she fought for my life, I lost my twin brother, my closest familial bond, and lifelong source of strength. This loss, while devastating and unimaginable, reshaped my healing, turning survival into something deeper than physical recovery. It became a journey of rebuilding my identity, trust, and purpose in the aftermath of profound trauma.

My writing offers an unfiltered perspective and a look into what it truly means to survive — not just the treatments and procedures, but the silent battles that follow, some ongoing. I reflect candidly about the loneliness, the emotional weight, and the resilience required to keep going when life no longer feels familiar.

Today, I use my voice and talents in Public Speaking to help others feel seen, heard, understood, and less alone. My writing and related work are deeply rooted in honesty, compassion, and the belief that even in the darkest moments, healing and meaning are still possible.
